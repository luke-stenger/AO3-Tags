Raw Results

These are the results of my analysis for Freeform tags on AO3:

The number to the left of each tag gives the ratio between the percent of M/M stories using that tag and the percent of F/M or F/F stories using that tag. For example a value of 2 means the tag is twice as likely to be in an M/M fic than a non-M/M fic. Intuitively, a value higher than 1 means the tag is more prevelant in M/M fics, and a value lower than 1 means the tag is less prevelant in M/M fics compared to other romance-focused fics. For more information, see the procedure section.

The tags are sorted here by overall popularity. I’ve displayed values for the 20,000 most popular tags. Near the bottom of the list, the tags become very specific and much less frequently used. Thus, their values carry less weight, as the sample size of stories is much smaller. Note that a value of exactly 0.000 means that not a single M/M fic used that tag, while a value of “inf.” (i.e. infinite correlation) means that the tag was only ever used in M/M fics.

Some Analysis

It's quite fun to peruse the list, and I encourage you to ctrl-f/cmd-f for some interesting tags to see how "BL-ish" they are on AO3. I will now focus in on some specific values and discuss their implications. Remember, these findings are very limited in scope (see Limitations).

The most obvious and strong correlations are seen when the tag directly references a genre of M/M or non M/M fiction:

39.49 - Slash 13.96 - Pre-Slash 0.035 - Femslash

Specific types of sex naturally have strong correlations...

43.85 - Gay Sex 0.227 - Heterosexual Sex 0.038 - Lesbian Sex

...as well as certain sex acts:

7.399 - Anal Sex 2.880 - Blow Jobs 8.165 - Anal Fingering 3.689 - Hand Jobs 7.867 - Frottage 0.777 - Oral Sex 0.123 - Cunnilingus 0.118 - Vaginal Fingering

And finally here are a couple more tags that are (very unsurprisingly) assosiated with M/M fics:

4.598 - Alpha/Beta/Omega Dynamics 32.63 - Mpreg

Next, we can look at some of our tropes from class, and see if they are actually disproportionately represented in M/M fics or not.

Dom/sub and hurt/comfort dynamics are indeed more prevelant in M/M fics, though not by as much as might be expected:

1.448 - Dom/sub 1.346 - Hurt/Comfort

These tropes from our shared document do seem to characterize M/M fics:

7.448 - Gay For You 5.299 - Crossdressing 2.823 - Blood Pacts 1.603 - Red String of Fate 1.523 - Sexual Abuse

...but these ones seem to have little correlation:

1.272 - Sharing a Bed 1.242 - Unrequited Love 0.996 - Enemies to Lovers

...and these ones actually seem to be more common in non M/M fics:

0.676 - Opposites Attract 0.645 - Forbidden Love 0.607 - Mentor/Protégé 0.558 - Love Triangles 0.442 - Tragic Romance 0.194 - Female Antagonist

Notably, female antagonists are much more represented in F/M or F/F fics compared to M/M fics, likely due to their role as romantic rivals.

Finally, the AO3 data does seem to support our observations on homosocial environments:

1.691 - Alternate Universe - Boarding School 1.534 - Rugby 2.302 - Football 3.650 - Minecraft 1.482 - Ice Skating
